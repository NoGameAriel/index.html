## What CPT Is

Condition Preservation Theory is the study of whether systems can sustain their enabling conditions without displacing degradation across hidden boundaries, and the production of instruments that make that constraint measurable, designable, and enforceable.

CPT is not a framework competing within existing disciplines.  
It is a precondition filter — a prior constraint that all other system design fields must pass through.

-----

## The Foundational Axiom

> A system must maintain the integrity of the conditions it depends on, without displacing degradation across time, space, or participants.

**“Integrity of the conditions it depends on”** — not just the system’s outputs, not just its internal health, but the upstream conditions. A different level of accountability than most frameworks reach.

**“Without displacing degradation”** — displacement is the mechanism. Extraction doesn’t destroy value; it moves the cost somewhere less visible.

**“Across time, space, or participants”** — the three axes of displacement.

- Temporal: debt and depletion
- Spatial: externality and offshoring
- Participant: burnout, exploitation, mandatory-depleting participation

-----

## The Eight Axioms

Each axiom closes a specific structural opening that extractive systems exploit.

### Axiom 1 — Regeneration is a Hard Constraint

A system cannot be considered viable if it depletes the conditions that sustain it. If inputs degrade faster than they recover, the system is structurally invalid — regardless of current output.

*Closes the viability loophole.*

### Axiom 2 — Externalities Must Be Internalized

A system cannot export its costs to unseen or unaccounted domains. If harm exists, it must be measured, attributed, and fed back into decision-making. Otherwise extraction hides. It doesn’t disappear.

*Closes the externalization loophole.*

### Axiom 3 — Signal Integrity is Prerequisite to Coordination

A system cannot function coherently if its feedback signals are distorted. If the system rewards misrepresentation, it will optimize for illusion, not reality.

*Closes the signal corruption loophole.*

### Axiom 4 — Throughput Cannot Exceed Adaptive Capacity

Any system has a rate at which it can process, respond, and adapt. When growth, production, or decision velocity exceeds that capacity, breakdown is inevitable. This is where many systems quietly become extractive: they scale faster than they can stay coherent.

*Closes the scale loophole.*

### Axiom 5 — Power Must Remain Revisable

If control within a system cannot be challenged, updated, or redistributed, extraction will emerge — not as moral failure, but as structural inevitability. Authority must be auditable. Decisions must be contestable. Capture must be detectable and correctable.

*Closes the capture loophole.*

### Axiom 6 — Participation Must Not Degrade the Participant

If engaging with a system leaves workers, users, or citizens less resourced, less aware, or less capable — the system is extractive, regardless of output. A valid system increases capacity through participation, not drains it.

*Closes the participation loophole.*

### Axiom 7 — Value Must Map to Real-World Effects

If a system’s internal rewards diverge from real-world outcomes, extraction emerges. A system must reward what actually sustains the broader field it operates within.

*Closes the reward divergence loophole.*

### Axiom 8 — Collapse Signals Must Be Legible Early

If a system only reveals failure at the point of crisis, it guarantees overshoot. Degradation must be visible before it becomes catastrophic. Early warnings must be actionable, not ignorable.

*Closes the legibility loophole.*

## The Six Rejection Conditions

A system is not admissible under CPT if any of the following are structurally true.

These are not design weaknesses. They are structural impossibilities under condition preservation logic.

### RC-1 — Unbounded Cost Displacement

If a system can operate only by exporting degradation to other populations, other ecosystems, future time periods, or unmeasured domains → it fails CPT immediately.

### RC-2 — Non-Observable Dependency Structure

If a system’s survival depends on inputs that cannot be identified, cannot be measured, or are intentionally obscured → it is not structurally legible enough to evaluate.

*If you can’t see what it eats, you can’t validate it.*

### RC-3 — Feedback Suppression or Distortion

If a system punishes accurate reporting of its effects, rewards misrepresentation of outcomes, or breaks the link between action and consequence → it becomes epistemically invalid.

*Without truthful feedback, no preservation test is possible.*

### RC-4 — Irreversible Power Asymmetry

If decision-making power cannot be challenged, cannot be revised, or cannot be redistributed under any condition → the system accumulates unchecked extraction risk by default.

*Non-revisable power always drifts toward condition degradation.*

### RC-5 — Throughput Exceeds Adaptive Capacity at Baseline

If the system is designed such that load > ability to respond, growth > ability to adapt, or complexity > ability to maintain coherence → collapse is structurally baked in, not accidental.

*This is not about scale. It’s about mismatch.*

### RC-6 — Dependency Violation

If the system degrades the conditions that allow its own continuation — the social, ecological, or informational substrate it relies on → it is self-contradictory under CPT.

*It eats the ground it stands on. This is the purest CPT violation.*

## The Constraint Refinement

Not: *no cost transfer ever* — too absolute, vulnerable to trivial counterexample.

But: **no unaccounted or non-recoverable cost transfer across system boundaries.**

Transfer that is visible, attributed, and recoverable is not extraction.  
Transfer that is hidden, unattributed, or permanent is.

## What CPT Produces

### System Legitimacy Diagnoses (SLD)

Not “is this system good or bad” — but: does it preserve the conditions that make its own continuation possible, without displacement? Produces a graded output: condition-preserving, condition-neutral, or condition-degrading.

CPT as falsification engine for systemic viability.

### Displacement Maps (DM)

Every system produces hidden transfers — ecological to economic, labor to productivity, attention to revenue, stability to growth. CPT makes displacement legible as a primary object, not a side-effect.

A theory of where systems hide their cost structure.

### Constraint-Based Design Grammars (CBDG)

Instead of prescribing solutions, CPT generates allowed structures, forbidden structures, and failure modes under scaling. Design prohibition logic, not design inspiration.

### Meta-System Auditing Tools (MSAT)

Instruments including Condition Dependency Registers, Extraction Vectors, Integrity Drift Scores, and Feedback Fidelity Indexes.

## CPT as Precondition Filter

CPT does not compete with existing disciplines. It precedes them.

- **Economics** becomes a subsystem subject to CPT constraints
- **Policy** becomes implementation under CPT validation
- **Technology** becomes infrastructure that must pass CPT thresholds
- **Governance** becomes maintenance of condition integrity

## The Two-Layer Epistemic Architecture

**Level 1 — CPT Gate (Pass/Fail)**
Six rejection conditions. Determines admissibility. No gradients. Binary at the boundary.

**Level 2 — CPT Stability Field (Gradient)**
Eight axioms. Measures drift within admissible systems. Produces risk trajectories.

**Level 3 — F&S Instrumentation**
Maps gradient signals into actionable diagnostics. Identifies intervention points before CPT failure.

The system only works if CPT is binary at the boundary and gradient inside the boundary.

## The Formal Skeleton

For each axiom *i* (i = 1…8):

- **sᵢ ∈ [0,1]** — current integrity state
- **τᵢ ∈ [0,1]** — threshold below which rejection condition activates
- **Dᵢ = sᵢ − τᵢ** — drift score (positive = inside stability field, negative = gate triggered)
- **Ḋᵢ** — rate of change (sign and magnitude both matter)

**Gate condition:** If any Dᵢ < 0 → system non-admissible. Analysis stops.

Phase 3 formalization will refine τᵢ values empirically from Gate Instrument behavioral data.
