Answer four questions about any verification system — a product, institution, regulator, media outlet, or certifier. You'll get a plain-language verdict and a shareable result.
Step 1
Name the system you're reading.
Examples: "my home inspector," "the FDA's supplement oversight," "peer review at this journal," "this news outlet's coverage of pharma"
Step 2 — The Four Questions
```
01
Who funds the verifier?
Does the money come from the entity being verified, or from an ecosystem that benefits from favorable outcomes?
02
Who controls their future work?
Even when payment is clean, referrals, reputation, and reappointment are leverage. Does the subject or its network control the verifier's pipeline?
03
How quickly does reality correct the system?
Captured systems often look clean until failure. Do errors surface through internal feedback, or only through crisis and external pressure?
04
Is disagreement preserved?
Healthy verification produces variance. Do reviewers dissent, flag problems, and contradict each other — or does everything consistently align?
```
