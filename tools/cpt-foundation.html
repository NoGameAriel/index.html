Foundational Axiom
A system must maintain the integrity of the conditions it depends on, without displacing degradation across time, space, or participants.
01

The Eight Axioms

Extractive systems survive by exploiting specific structural openings. Each axiom closes one. Together they map the complete attack surface of condition degradation.

AXIOM 01
Regeneration is a hard constraint, not an outcome

A system cannot be considered viable if it depletes the conditions that sustain it. If inputs degrade faster than they recover, the system is structurally invalid — regardless of current output.

This flips sustainability from branding to operational requirement.

Closes the viability loophole
AXIOM 02
Externalities must be internalized

A system cannot export its costs to unseen or unaccounted domains. Environmental damage, psychological burnout, social fragmentation — if harm exists, it must be measured, attributed, and fed back into decision-making.

Otherwise extraction hides. It doesn't disappear.

Closes the externalization loophole
AXIOM 03
Signal integrity is prerequisite to coordination

A system cannot function coherently if its feedback signals are distorted. This includes price signals, metrics, and communication. If the system rewards misrepresentation, it will optimize for illusion, not reality.

Closes the signal corruption loophole
AXIOM 04
Throughput cannot exceed adaptive capacity

Any system has a rate at which it can process, respond, and adapt. When growth, production, or decision velocity exceeds that capacity, breakdown is inevitable. This is where many systems quietly become extractive: they scale faster than they can stay coherent.

Closes the scale loophole
AXIOM 05
Power must remain revisable

If control within a system cannot be challenged, updated, or redistributed, extraction will emerge — not as moral failure, but as structural inevitability. Authority must be auditable. Decisions must be contestable. Capture must be detectable and correctable.

Closes the capture loophole
AXIOM 06
Participation must not degrade the participant

If engaging with a system leaves workers, users, or citizens less resourced, less aware, or less capable — the system is extractive, regardless of output. A valid system increases capacity through participation, not drains it.

Closes the participation loophole
AXIOM 07
Value must map to real-world effects

If a system's internal rewards diverge from real-world outcomes, extraction emerges. Profit without ecological accounting. Engagement without well-being. Growth without stability. A system must reward what actually sustains the broader field it operates within.

Closes the reward divergence loophole
AXIOM 08
Collapse signals must be legible early

If a system only reveals failure at the point of crisis, it guarantees overshoot. Degradation must be visible before it becomes catastrophic. Early warnings must be actionable, not ignorable. Otherwise systems run until they break.

Closes the legibility loophole
02

What CPT Produces

A useful discipline is defined less by its claims and more by its outputs under constraint. CPT produces four distinct artifact types that no existing systems field is currently generating.

SLD
System Legitimacy Diagnoses
Not "is this system good or bad" — but: does it preserve the conditions that make its own continuation possible, without displacement? Produces a graded output: condition-preserving, condition-neutral, or condition-degrading. CPT as falsification engine for systemic viability.
DM
Displacement Maps
Every system produces hidden transfers — ecological to economic, labor to productivity, attention to revenue, stability to growth. CPT makes displacement legible as a primary object, not a side-effect. A theory of where systems hide their cost structure.
CBDG
Constraint-Based Design Grammars
Instead of prescribing solutions, CPT generates allowed structures, forbidden structures, and failure modes under scaling. Design prohibition logic, not design inspiration. Architecturally powerful because it operates at the level of what cannot be built.
MSAT
Meta-System Auditing Tools
Instruments including Condition Dependency Registers, Extraction Vectors, Integrity Drift Scores, and Feedback Fidelity Indexes. Where the diagnostic instinct becomes operational infrastructure.
03

Discipline Definition

CPT defined

Condition Preservation Theory is the study of whether systems can sustain their enabling conditions without displacing degradation across hidden boundaries, and the production of instruments that make that constraint measurable, designable, and enforceable.

04

Scope & Precondition Filter

CPT is not another framework competing within existing disciplines. It functions as a precondition filter — a prior constraint that all other system design fields must pass through.

Economics Becomes a subsystem subject to CPT constraints. Economic models must account for condition preservation before optimization.
Policy Becomes implementation under CPT validation. Policy design must demonstrate non-displacement before adoption.
Technology Becomes infrastructure that must pass CPT thresholds. Scaling decisions require adaptive capacity assessment.
Governance Becomes maintenance of condition integrity. Governance legitimacy is measured by preservation, not control.

Not: no cost transfer ever — too absolute, vulnerable to trivial counterexample.

But: no unaccounted or non-recoverable cost transfer across system boundaries.

This preserves realism while keeping the constraint intact. Transfer that is visible, attributed, and recoverable is not extraction. Transfer that is hidden, unattributed, or permanent is.

05

Current Implementation

Domain-specific implementation · Institutional coherence

Field & Signal is the first operational implementation of Condition Preservation Theory, demonstrating how CPT constraints manifest in institutional coherence systems and can be operationalized into diagnostic and design instruments.

F&S demonstrates that CPT is not hypothetical. The diagnostic stack — Loop Integrity Architecture, Conditions Layer, Field Reading instruments, Cross-Scale Check — constitutes working proof that post-extractive system design can be instrumented and deployed at the practitioner level.
